## Why It Matters

### The Developer Productivity Crisis

Modern software development faces a critical challenge: **cognitive overload**. Developers spend:
- **35% of time** searching documentation
- **25% of time** debugging and troubleshooting  
- **20% of time** writing boilerplate code
- **15% of time** understanding legacy code
- **Only 5%** on innovative, high-value work

**Our Solution:** Shift 75% of routine cognitive tasks to AI assistance, allowing developers to focus on architecture, innovation, and business logic.

### The Vendor Lock-In Problem

Current AI coding assistants force organizations into single-vendor relationships:
- **GitHub Copilot** - Microsoft ecosystem only
- **Tabnine** - Limited model selection
- **Amazon CodeWhisperer** - AWS-centric
- **Cursor/Continue** - VS Code exclusive

**Our Solution:** Provider-agnostic architecture enabling organizations to:
- Compare AI models for specific tasks
- Switch providers based on cost, performance, or compliance
- Maintain competitive leverage in vendor negotiations
- Avoid dependency on single AI provider's availability

### The NetBeans Opportunity Gap

NetBeans IDE serves **2.1 million developers globally** (2025 JetBrains Developer Survey), yet receives minimal AI tooling investment compared to VS Code and JetBrains IDEs.

**Our Solution:** First-class AI assistance for an underserved but significant developer community, particularly strong in:
- Enterprise Java development
- Educational institutions
- Government and regulated industries
- Emerging markets
